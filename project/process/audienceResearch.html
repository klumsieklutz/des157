Ia Maria Lo

Student at UC Davis

First generation college student

Ia Lo is a student a Hmong student at UC Davis majoring in anthropology and minors in education. At UC Davis she is the cultural chair for the Hmong Student Union. Recently she has coordinated two cultral show. She is fluent in both written and oral of the Hmong and English language and is heavily involve in the Hmong community in Sacramento. During the summer she participates in a summer program teaching Hmong youth the Hmong language and culture.

After graduating from UC Davis, she intends to go to the Sacramento State education program where she can get her credentials to become a teacher. She wants to be teacher in the social science field teaching elementary to middle schoolers and return back to her community in Sacramento.

She is the youngest of a family of 10, a typical number for a Hmong family. Because she is the youngest, there are many expectations for her to be better than her older siblings. Since she is the youngest, she has a responsibility to take care of her aging father who is well known elder in the Hmong community. Because of her father's status in the community, she is very family oriented and seeks ways to help her family as much as possible whether physically, emotionally, or financially.

She also has a loving boyfriend who supports her through thick and thin. Although his level of education is not as high as her's, she challenges him academically to pursue a better life. She does not let the status of education get in the way of her love for him.
